Research

My research experience spans topics in environmental and development economics, neighborhood dynamics, poverty, and forced migration. Across these roles, I have supported faculty and supervisors alike through data cleaning, analysis, and visualization of results for presentations and publications using Stata, R, and ArcGIS Pro, often working with large administrative datasets and spatial data.

Research Experience

  • Research Assistant, Dr. Anna Maria Santiago, Michigan State University, 2022–present
    Conduct data analysis and spatial modeling on poverty, neighborhood effects, and low-income homeownership using Stata, R, and ArcGIS Pro. Contributed to one published article and several co-authored manuscripts under review, while also gaining experience with large-scale national housing datasets.

  • Research Assistant, Dr. Prabhat Barnwal, Michigan State University, Summer 2022
    Supported projects in development and environmental economics. Cleaned and analyzed large-scale administrative datasets (10M+ observations) and developed efficient, reproducible workflows in Stata and R.

  • Intern, United Nations Refugee Agency (UNHCR), Middle East North Africa (MENA) Bureau, Geneva, Switzerland, Spring 2017
    Analyzed budgetary data for UNHCR’s North Africa operations (2013–2017). Managed and responded to individual queries from displaced individuals in the MENA region and developed a proposal to streamline UNHCR’s response process.
